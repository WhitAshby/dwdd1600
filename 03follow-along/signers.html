Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most Famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Primary author of the Declaration of Independence. Later became the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
A key advocate for independence. Later became the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
One of the most influential Founding Fathers. Known for his diplomacy, inventions, and contributions to science and literature.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress at the time. Famous for his large, bold signature on the Declaration.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
A prominent political activist and leader in the American Revolution. Known for organizing resistance to British policies, including the Boston Tea Party.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
Only person to sign all four of the U.S. founding documents: the Continental Association, Declaration of Independence, Articles of Confederation, and the Constitution.
Robert Morris (Pennsylvania)
A major financier of the American Revolution.
Richard Henry Lee (Virginia)
Introduced the resolution calling for independence from Britain.

Each of these inviduals played a signigicant role in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in the signing the Declaration, as act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
